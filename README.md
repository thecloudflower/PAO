## Current status

**Personal AI OS**, or **PAO**, is currently in private pre-alpha development.

PAO is being built as a local-first environment where personal context, knowledge, projects, and AI-assisted workflows can remain coherent over months and years.

The active source code, internal documentation, development history, private operational context, and vault materials are not public at this time.

This repository serves as PAO’s public landing page only.

## What does PAO actually do?

In simple terms, PAO is being designed as a private digital home for the important parts of your life.

It can help you keep track of:

- what you are working on
- what you have learned
- decisions you have made
- goals you are trying to reach
- information you may need again later

Instead of starting over every time you open an AI assistant, PAO is designed to help the system understand the context you choose to provide and continue from where you left off.

For example, PAO could help you return to a project months later and remember:

- what the project was about
- what had already been completed
- what decisions were made
- which information supported those decisions
- what the next step was supposed to be

PAO is not meant to take control of your life or make important decisions for you. It is meant to help you stay organized, remember what matters, and make more informed decisions yourself.

You remain in control of what PAO can see, what it can use, and what actions it is allowed to take.

## What PAO explores

PAO explores how a personal system can safely understand context, preserve continuity, and adapt as the person using it grows.

Current areas of development include:

- local-first personal knowledge management
- structured context and memory handoff
- AI-assisted project continuity
- explicit permission and trust boundaries
- reviewable information retrieval
- source provenance and citation tracking
- human-approved automation
- versioned personal operating workflows
- long-term personal growth infrastructure

## What makes PAO different

PAO is not intended to be an autonomous system that silently makes decisions on someone’s behalf.

Its foundations are designed around:

- **Local control** — personal information remains under the user’s authority.
- **Explicit context** — the user decides what information enters an active session.
- **Review before action** — consequential steps require clear human approval.
- **Visible evidence** — retrieval reasons, freshness, duplication, and provenance can be reviewed.
- **Continuity over time** — projects, decisions, and personal context can survive across sessions.
- **Adaptation without replacement** — the system supports human judgment rather than replacing it.

## Development philosophy

PAO is built around a simple principle:

> Personal systems should adapt as the person grows.

The goal is not to automate a person’s life or replace human judgment. The goal is to provide clearer context, safer workflows, stronger continuity, and a more useful relationship between people and their tools.
